Olympian Gods

Aphrodite

The Olympian goddess of love, beauty and fertility.

Zeus

The Olympian god of the sky and the thunder, the king of all other gods and men, and, consequently, the chief figure in Greek mythology.

Poseidon

The violent and ill-tempered god of the sea.

Apollo

The Olympian god of the sun and light, music and poetry, healing and plagues, prophecy and knowledge, order and beauty, archery and agriculture.

Ares

The Olympian god of war.

Artemis

The Olympian goddess of the hunt, the moon, and chastity; in time, she also became associated with childbirth and nature.

Athena

Athena is the Olympian goddess of wisdom and war and the adored patroness of the city of Athens.

Hestia

Hestia is the Greek goddess of the hearth, one of the original Twelve Olympians.

Hermes

Hermes is the winged herald and messenger of the Olympian gods.

Hera

Queen of the gods and the goddess of marriage, women, childbirth and family.

Hephaestus

Master blacksmith and craftsman of the gods; god of the forge, craftsmanship, invention, fire and volcanoes.

Demeter

oddess of the harvest, fertility, agriculture, nature and the seasons.

Titans

Asteria

A Titan goddess, daughter of the Titans Coeus and Phoebe and sister of Leto. She was married to the Titan Perses, and had a daughter, Hecate.

Astraeus

A Titan god in Greek mythology, son of the Titans Crius and Eurybia. He was the god of the dusk and the winds.

Atlas

The leader of the Titan rebellion against Zeus, and he got a fitting punishment after the end of the Titanomachy: he was condemned to eternally hold up the sky.

Clymene

A Titan goddess in Greek mythology, daughter of the Titans Oceanus and Tethys, thus making her an Oceanid. She was the goddess of renown, fame, and infamy.

Coeus

Coeus was one of the Titans, son of Uranus and Gaia.

Crius

Crius was one of the Titans, son of Uranus and Gaea. He married Eurybia, daughter of Pontus and Gaea, and had three children, Astraios, Pallas and Perses.
